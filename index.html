Character Role Card

At the start of each round, each player would draw a role card.
The roles are:

  • Innocent: Your job is to solve the puzzle!
  • Traitor: Your job is to either confirm all the innocents have died or destroy 5 puzzle pieces.
  • (Optional) Jester Role: Your job is to get your self killed, but you can't do damage.
    If you are killed, swap role cards with your killer.
  • (Optional) Brown Role:
  • (Optional) Detective Role: You are an innocent. When you succeed on an investigation check of a body, you can reveal the role card to everyone.

Wands!

When travelling around the map you might find a wand.
There are two types of wands: Attack Wands and Utility Wands.
When you find a wand, click on a wand card. One will appear in your 'inventory' space above.
Click on that image to display the spell. (spell card may appear far to the right, just scroll over)











Solving the puzzle

As you travel around the map you will collect puzzle pieces. Once you have revealed the Puzzle Room, you can try to solve the puzzle.
You need five puzzle pieces to be placed in the Puzzle Room to attempt to solve the puzzle.
Hopefully I will design a cool little puzzle thing with a variety of options and solutions.
Or it will just be a win condition.
The traitors can do something similar. They can win by destroying five puzzle pieces.
As with the innocents, the puzzle pieces must first be placed in the puzzle room.

Who won?

After some number of rounds, the player with the highest individual score wins.

Rules of the Game

Phases

Movement All players place one movement card face down
Everyone turns their cards over at the same time
Move all player tokens
Add map tiles where required
Map tiles must be joined door to door
Action
All players place one action card in front of them
Everyone turns over their cards at the same time
Immediately resolve single-player actions
Resolution
Resolve Question actions
Choose question | Roll corresponding dice | Update suspicions
Resolve Attack actions
Place wand cards | Attack wand effect | Utility wand effect
Update HP | Suspicions | Point Counters
Any players in an Attack gain Mandatory Movement condition
(Note: Both wand effects occur before HP is reduced)

Set Up

Each player selects a character sheet (CS)

Starting Tokens
1d6 on 'HP' square of CS (6 up)
Suspicion tokens next to each player on CS (Green up)
Place Marker

Starting Cards
Set the desired rolls for the round:
#Traitors | #Innocent | #Pink Roles | #Brown Roles
Shuffle and deal out Role Cards, one to each player
Role cards are kept secret (Unless exposed)
Shuffle Side Quest cards. Every player draws one
Side Quest cards are kept secret (Until completed)
Shuffle Wand cards and place in two piles (Face down):
Attack Wands (Red) | Utility Wands (Orange)

Starting Map Tiles
Place the Ballroom tile in the middle of the table
Shuffle Room tiles and place in pile (face down)

Game Objectives

The game is played in multiple rounds
At the end of the game, the player with the most points wins

Points are earned by completing Quests:
Main Quests are based on Player Role
Side Quests are drawn at the beginning of each round

Each Round is played in Phases
A round ends when:
Traitors win | Innocents win

Game lengths vary
Either play a certain # of rounds,
Play inderterminately until you are out of time,
Establish a Number of Points to win

Deciding Player Roles
Group can decide on #Innocents, #Traitors, etc.
Or rotate a 'Dealer' each round
Dealer chooses the role make-up at the start of each round

Status Conditions

Manditory Movement
Triggered by Attack Action
Both players MUST move out of the room
in the next movement phase Applies only to room where the Attack action happened
If a utility wand moved a player:
Player may choose to stay in that room next round

Suspicious | Trusted
All players start out Trusting everyone (green token)
Innocents can only Attack a player you suspect
Gain suspicion by:
Investigating a body
Witnessing a Murder
Witnessing a Destroy action
Questioning
Investigation

Actions

Investigate
Investigating a body reveals:
Role: Investigating player can look at the dead player's role card
Investigator gains any puzzle pieces on the body
Possible identity of the Killer:
Roll d20 + bonus
Total < 10: Roll a d6, count # players clockwise around table
Become Suspicious of that player
10 < Total < 15: Learn who killed them
Become Suspicious of the killer
Total > 15: Learn player's role and killer's identity
Player can look at the Role Card of the victim
Player becomes suspicious of killer

Question
When in the same room as another player
Choose 1 question:
Are you Innocent? | Are you Traitor?
Roll d20 + Bonus
Total < 5: Target can cast Suspicion or Trust
Target flips one Suspicion/Trust token
on Questioner's CS
5 < Total < 15: Target provides a Yes/No answer
Target may lie or tell the truth
Questioner updates Suspicion/Trust accordingly
Total > 15: Questioner looks at Target's Role Card
Target does NOT have to answer the question
Target can choose to answer and may lie
Search
After moving into a room, can take Search action
Choose to search for:
Puzzle Piece | Attack Wand | Utility Wand
Roll a d20 + Bonus:
Total > 10: Find what you wanted
Draw card and place face down by your CS
You are not allowed to look at any card until it is played
Total < 10: Find what you didn't want
Draw card from one of other piles
Do not look at the card until it is played
Note: Can only Search immediately after entering a room
Each player can only Search each room ONCE
(Either place a token or try to remember)
Attack
After moving into same room as another player
Can take the Attack action
Attacking immediately grants Suspicious
Target's Action choice is overrided by the Attack action
If two or more player choose Attack:
Both players must play Attack Wands (or unarmed)
Attacker(s) choose(s): Attack Wand | Unarmed Attack
Target chooses: Utility Wand | Do nothing
Place wand cards on table and turn over simultaneosly
Resolve BOTH wands before reducing HP
Attack wand(s) effect(s) | Utility wand effect | Reduce HP
v Place used Wand Cards on the discard pile
Each Wand Card can only be used once
All wand effects always happen, unless impossible

Note: If a Utility Wand causes a player to change locations
Change location then reduce HP
This may result in a player dying in a different room
Both players gain Mandatory Movement condition
Puzzle
For Innocents and Traitors:
When in the Puzzle Room can take Puzzle action
Action first places all acquired puzzle pieces in the room
If 5 or more puzzle pieces have been placed:
Traitors can attempt to Destroy pieces
Innocent can attempt to Solve the Puzzle

Destroying Puzzle Pieces
If there are no non-innocent players in the puzzle room
All placed pieces are destroyed
Traitors then win the Round
If there is a non-innocent player in the room:
Triggers an Attack action from the non-innocent
Attack interrupts the Destroy action
After resolving the Attack gain Mandatory Movment
Traitors cannot play Attack Wands only Utility Wands

Solving the Puzzle
Must actually solve the puzzle
If cannot, may need more pieces before trying again

Death and Ghosts

When a player is reduced to 0HP, they are dead
Flip character token over and leave it in place

Ghost Player Actions
Dead players become Poltergeists
Place a Ghost Token in the Ballroom upon death
Ghosts can move but cannot place new map tiles

Ghosts have one action: Haunt
Must declare during Action Phase
When in the same room as another player making a roll
Can choose to Roll d20
Roll < 15: Nothing happens
Roll ≥ 15: Apply a buff or debuff = ±2
(De)buff can be applied to ANY dice roll
Including damage rolls
Can only be applied to one dice roll

After successfully Haunting, gain Mandatory Movement

Player Roles

Innocent
Main Quest: Innocents win by solving the puzzle
To solve the puzzle, 5 pieces must be found around the map
In the Puzzle Room, can take the Puzzle Action
Puzzle action places puzzle pieces in the room
After 5 pieces are placed can Solve the puzzle
If solved, the round ends and the innocent's win
If not, can reattempt with subsequent actions
Puzzle pieces cannot be picked up from the ground

When a player dies with pieces
if victum dies in front of their killer:
Pieces transfer to the killer
If victim dies in a separate room:
Any player who Investigates the room gains the pieces

Attacking
Innocents cannot take Attack action against a player they Trust
Can only attack a Suspicious player with an Attack Wand
Traitor
Main Quest: Traitors have two win conditions
1. If all innocent are confirmed to be dead
2. If 5 pieces are placed in the puzzle room and Destroyed

Destroying puzzle pieces
In the Puzzle Room, Puzzle action places down pieces
Instead of Solving the Puzzle, traitors can Destroy pieces:
Must be 5 pieces or more placed in the room
There cannot be any non-traitor (witness) in the room

If a traitor tries to destroy pieces with a wittness:
Triggers an Attack from the witness (Gain Suspicious)
Attack interrupts the Destroy action
Traitor can:
Attack Wand | Utility Wand | Do nothing
Witnesses can:
Attack Wand | Utility Wand | Do nothing
Traitor and Witness gain Mandatory Movement condition

Special Traits
Traitors do not need to be Suspicious to take Attack action
Traitors can Attack without an Attack Wand
Unarmed Attack does 1d4 of damage

Confirming an innocent has died:
Investigating a room with a body
Rolling > 15 on check
May have already learned a player was innocent

If Traitors think they have won, the need to declare victory
If there is an innocent still alive, the Traitors loose
and the Innocents win

If in doubt, get a deceased player to check and verify all roles
Jester
Main Quest: The Jester's role is to get a player to kill them
When another player kills a Jester, the player dies
The Jester then assumes the role of that player,
and the player becomes a ghost, with the jester role
(Swap role cards)
If this happens, the Jester gains 3 Points

Special Characterstics of Jesters:
Jesters cannot do damage by any means
If a jester uses an attack wand, the wand's effects happen
but the damage dealt will be zero.
